## About Neighborhood Moderators on Nextdoor

Neighborhood moderators (Leads and Review Team members) are neighbors who have volunteered to help [moderate the conversations](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/About-moderation?language=en_US) in their neighborhoods. When posts or comments are reported, moderators vote on whether those posts or comments should be removed or stay on the site. Neighborhood Leads and Review team members are often seen as leaders in their local communities, both on and off Nextdoor. Participation in either program is completely optional, though if a neighbor chooses to opt-in, we do expect adherence to our [Community Programs Code of Conduct](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Nextdoor-Community-Programs-Code-of-Conduct?language=en_US).

**Please Note**

Leads do not have the ability to remove neighbors, or otherwise affect a neighbor's account in any way. Only Nextdoor's Support team can take those actions.

### FAQs

In communities where more moderators are needed, Nextdoor's system will invite a random sample of qualified neighbors. Neighbors qualify by being active on Nextdoor and having low number of reports, mutes, or messages removed for guidelines violations.

Neighbors who are _not_ moderators will _not_ be able to see a list or any indication that someone is a moderator in their neighborhood. _Only moderators can see who other moderators are._

## Review and Remove Reported Content as a Moderator

Leads and community reviewers are notified when content has been reported by neighbors and needs review. Then, Leads and community reviewers vote on whether or not reported content is in violation of the [Community Guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines) and should be removed from Nextdoor. Nextdoor takes action on reported content based on these votes.

**1. Navigate to the Review Tool**

- **Web**
  1. Click on your **profile photo or initials** in the top right corner
  2. Click **View profile**.
  3. Under dashboard, select **[Lead tools](https://nextdoor.com/lead_tools/).**
  4. Click **Reported content** beneath For your review.

- **iPhone**
  1. Open the **Nextdoor for iPhone** app.
  2. Tap your **profile photo or initials** in the top left corner.
  3. Under dashboard, tap **Lead tools.**
  4. Tap **Reported content**.

- **Android**
  1. Open the **Nextdoor for Android** app.
  2. Tap your **profile photo or initials** in the top left corner.
  3. Under dashboard, tap **Lead tools.**
  4. Tap **Reported content**.

**2. Determine if content violates the Guidelines with these evaluation tools:**

- Click the **title** to see the full conversation.
- **[Private message](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Best-practices-Private-messages)** the author to suggest they [edit their content](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-edit-a-post). Oftentimes, an author may not be aware of the Guidelines.
- Click the relevant [Community Guideline](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines) listed after **Reported as violating:** to clarify whether the reported content does violate the Guidelines.
- Click **Show** to see the content's history.

**3. Vote**

- Click **Vote.**
- Select **Remove** if the content is in violation of the Guidelines and should immediately be removed.
- Select **Keep** if the content does not violate the Guidelines and should remain.
- Select **Maybe remove** if you're not sure how to handle the post.

**REMINDER** Leads should only vote **Remove** on content that violates the [Community Guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines) or [Member Agreement](https://nextdoor.com/member_agreement/). Voting to remove content that does not violate policy is itself a violation.

**IMPORTANT NOTE** Leads should not reveal the identity of a content reporter to the member whose content was reported.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**When should I remove content?**

- Only remove posts that violate the [Community Guidelines](https://nextdoor.com/neighborhood_guidelines/#guidelines) or [Member Agreement](https://nextdoor.com/member_agreement/). Removing posts that do not violate policy is itself a violation of policy.
- Do not remove old or expired posts from your Newsfeed. The Newsfeed serves as an archive of your neighborhood's activity.
- For Lost & Found posts that have expired, you can [close the discussion](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-close-a-discussion?language=en_US).

**What happens to content after I report it or vote that it be removed?**

Content that receives reports or votes to remove may get decreased visibility or be removed from Nextdoor entirely based on factors that include the number and type of reports a piece of content receives, how Leads vote on these reports, and Nextdoor's automated detection systems. Note that content may be removed from one neighborhood while still being visible in other neighborhoods.

**Where can I see my past votes?**

After you've voted, the content will be moved to the [History tab](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/moderation-history-tool?language=en_US) for reference.

**How many votes does it take to remove content?**

There is no fixed number. There are many factors that can potentially affect how many votes it takes to remove content including the number of times it was reported, the reporting reasons, the category or subject of the content, whether the content is similar to content that has been reported in the past, the history of the poster (and of the reporters and voters), as well as other factors.

**Are there other ways that content may be removed?**

Yes. The poster of the content or Nextdoor Support can remove content at any time. Note as well that when a post or thread is removed, all replies to that post or thread are automatically removed as well.

[Learn more about moderation here.](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/About-moderation)

## Resources and Support for Moderators

We want conversations on Nextdoor to reflect the nature of neighborhoods in the real world, and that's where the neighborhood leads and reviewers come in. As neighborhood moderators, you all have the local knowledge and expertise that makes your voice critical in knowing if a piece of reported content is truly hurtful or harmful to your neighborhood. Your willingness to contribute to your community in this way sets you up as a local leader.

### Education & Training Resources

- [Moderator Academy](https://www.nextdoorneighborhoodteams.com/home/collections/moderator-academy): Available in the Resource Hub, Moderator Academy is designed to help Leads and Reviewers build their moderation skills.
- [The Resource Hub](https://www.nextdoorneighborhoodteams.com/home): The Resource Hub is our home base for neighborhood moderators.
- [Peer Collaboration Groups](https://www.nextdoorneighborhoodteams.com/home/tools): Join a local or national group on Nextdoor to meet other neighborhood moderators.
- [Anti-Racism Resources](https://about.nextdoor.com/antiracism/): We encourage neighborly discussions about racial justice, and anti-racism efforts.
- [The Loneliness Toolkit](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Nextdoor-Loneliness-Toolkit?language=en_US): Learn to recognize the signs of loneliness and disconnection.

In addition, continue to leverage the resources published in Nextdoor's Help Center. The Help Center is the sole source of truth for the [Community Guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines?language=en_US), and other important policies.

### Getting Help from Nextdoor Support

Nextdoor Support is available 7 days a week to respond to content escalations, neighbor profile reports, and other questions and concerns. Most support requests receive a response within 24 hours.

To report a neighbor to Nextdoor Support, follow the in-app reporting process, as described in this article: [How to report a neighbor](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-report-a-member?language=en_US).

### When to Contact Local Emergency Assistance

Nextdoor Support is not, and should not, be seen as a replacement for local emergency assistance.

If you are concerned about someone's well-being as a result of posts they may have made on Nextdoor, consider whether the situation calls for immediate help from the police, fire department or a [social service agency](https://www.usa.gov/state-social-services). While it isn't available nationally, many cities have a 311 system in place for non-emergency assistance, and [211](https://www.211.org/) for information and referrals to social services and other assistance.

Additional resources to support suicide & self-harm prevention are available in this article: [Suicide & Self-Harm Prevention](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Suicide-Self-Harm-Prevention?language=en_US).

## Toolkit: How to Moderate When a Crisis Hits Your Neighborhood

There are many different kinds of events that can challenge the strength of a neighborhood. For instance: natural disasters, serious criminal behavior and arrests, life-altering accidents, etc. Though painful to endure, these kinds of crises offer the opportunity for neighbors to come together and build stronger bonds.

### Scenario 1: General Incivility

Local incidents often cause a lot of shock, sadness, and anger within a neighborhood, and those feelings can often translate to an emotion-fueled post on Nextdoor.

**Recommended initial action:** Post a community guidelines reminder on the thread.

**Sample template:**

_Hi - While there is a lot of shock and surprise, sadness, anger, and hurt about [INCIDENT], I want to remind everyone of the [community guidelines.](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines) In particular, it's really important we treat one another with respect during times of tension._

**Follow up actions:**

- Report and/or vote to remove individual comments that violate the community guidelines
- If challenging comments continue, send a personal message to the original poster and request they close the thread
- Report and/or vote to remove the entire thread if it violates the [guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines?language=en_US)

### Scenario 2: Unverified Speculation and/or Gossip

**Recommended initial action:** Post a community guidelines reminder on the thread.

**Sample template:**

_Let's stay focused on facts so that we don't unintentionally make assumptions or generalizations in any comments or posts we make. Please keep the notion of "innocent until proven guilty" in mind as you discuss this unfortunate incident._

### Scenario 3: Violating Privacy of Individuals Involved

**Recommended initial action:** Report the post, or vote to remove the post under the category of a "Safety issue". Then, send a message to the original poster.

**Sample template:**

_Hi NAME - I wanted to reach out to you following your latest post. I know that [INCIDENT] has been very concerning, but it's also important to respect the privacy of all people involved. For more, see [Nextdoor's privacy guidelines.](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/keep-it-clean-and-legal?language=en_US#privacy)_

### Scenario 4: Victim Blaming or Shaming

Victim blaming is a violation of [Nextdoor's Community Guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Be-respectful-to-your-neighbors?language=en_US#shaming).

**Recommended initial action:** Report the post under Disrespectful > Public Shaming. If comfortable, post a comment reminding others why victim blaming is harmful.

**Follow up:** If the post isn't removed by neighborhood moderators in a timely fashion, [report it to Nextdoor Support](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/authcontactus?language=en_US).

### Scenario 5: Racial Profiling

Racial profiling is not tolerated on Nextdoor, as part of the [Do Not Discriminate](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Do-not-discriminate?language=en_US) guideline.

**Recommended initial action:** Immediately report the post under Discriminatory > Racial Profiling.

Reports of discrimination and racial profiling go directly to Nextdoor Support, so it should be removed promptly.

### Scenario 6: Ongoing Discussions About the Incident

**Recommended initial action:** Create a group, and post on relevant threads so that members know how to join. All groups must adhere to the [Group Guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-report-a-group?language=en_US).

## FAQ - Elections and Politics on Nextdoor

Elections are top of mind for neighbors around the world, so we want to provide resources, FAQs, and information on our policies around discussing national and local politics on Nextdoor.

### Resources

**Policies/What's allowed:**

- [Nextdoor's policies on local and national politics/issues](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Be-helpful-in-conversations?language=en_US#localnational)
- [Nextdoor's misinformation policy](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Do-not-engage-in-harmful-activity?language=en_US#misinformation)

**Reporting and moderation resources:**

- [Reasons for reporting posts or comments](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/reasons-for-reporting-content?language=en_US)
- [How to report a post or comment](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-report-content?language=en_US)
- [How to report a member](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-report-a-member?language=en_US)
- [How to report a group](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Report-a-group-on-Nextdoor)
- [About moderation](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/About-moderation?language=en_US)

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Are discussions about politics/elections allowed on Nextdoor?**

- **National politics/elections** are only allowed in Nextdoor Groups, not in the main feed.
- **Local politics/elections** are allowed anywhere on Nextdoor.

**How do you define local versus national politics/elections?**

- **"Local"** topics are those that affect the local community or state only.
- **"National"** topics are those that extend beyond the local area into national conversations.

**What are the rules regarding advocacy and campaigning?**

Posts about national campaigns are only allowed in groups. Local candidates are allowed to introduce themselves in the main feed. Follow-up campaign content needs to take place in a group.

**What are you doing to protect members from misinformation?**

Nextdoor uses technology and member reports to identify and remove content that violates the [election misinformation policy](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Be-helpful-in-conversations?language=en_US#misinformation).

**Who moderates political/election content on Nextdoor?**

A combination of technology and the human judgment of members, moderators, and the Nextdoor Neighborhood Operations team. [Learn more.](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/About-moderation?language=en_US)

**How do you ensure moderation is unbiased?**

Moderators commit to evaluating content according to the [Community Guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines?language=en_US). If you believe a moderator is unfairly reporting or voting on content, you can [report that member](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-report-a-member?language=en_US) for biased moderation.

## Toolkit: Managing Conversations About Elections and Politics

Political decisions impact each person in different ways. Nextdoor's Community Guidelines are in place to prevent the occurrence of harmful and/or hurtful content. As a reminder, please moderate to support the removal of any content that violates Nextdoor's guidelines, regardless of your personal feelings about the topic.

### Understanding Nextdoor's Election Policies

Nextdoor is committed to supporting a fair and safe election process. The [Do not engage in harmful activity guideline](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Do-not-engage-in-harmful-activity?language=en_US#misinformation) strictly prohibits:

- False or misleading claims that prevent or discourage people from voting
- Messages that call for or could incite interference with civil election processes
- False or misleading claims about election results
- Messages that call for or could incite violence to prevent a peaceful transfer of power

### Voter Suppression and Why It's Harmful

Voter suppression is any false or misleading actions or information that could prevent or discourage people from voting. Examples include:

- Sharing false information about when, where, or how to vote
- Attempts to prevent others from voting
- Calls for neighbors to interfere with the voting process

### Fact Checking Resources

- [Vote.org](http://vote.org/)
- [Ballotpedia.org](https://ballotpedia.org/Voter_identification_laws_by_state)
- [FactCheck.org](https://www.factcheck.org/search/#gsc.tab=0)
- [AP Fact Check](https://apnews.com/hub/ap-fact-check)
- [Secretary of State websites](https://www.e-secretaryofstate.com/)

### Tips for Civil Discussions During Election Cycles

- **Take care of yourself, first** — Make sure you're in a good place before engaging on controversial topics.
- **Find the shared humanity** — Assume positive intent in your neighbors.
- **Maintain self-awareness and neutrality** — Be mindful of your own words and tone.
- **Facilitate hard conversations instead of shutting them down** — Jump in early with positive comments to guide the conversation.
- **Stick to the guidelines** — Treat all posts and comments reasonably, and do not give favorable treatment to one issue/candidate over the other.

## Moderation on Nextdoor (Depth 2)

Nextdoor uses both technology and human review to detect and take action on content that violates the [community guidelines](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines?language=en_US) and terms of service, including:

- Algorithms that proactively detect harmful content
- Tools neighbors can use to report harmful content and people
- A team of neighborhood volunteer moderators who vote on content reports leveraging their local knowledge
- A team of trained Nextdoor employees who review flagged content and profiles

Most reported content goes to volunteer neighborhood moderators for review. Reports that are more sensitive in nature—misinformation, discrimination, etc.—go directly to Nextdoor Support.

### About Neighborhood Moderators

Nextdoor's community moderators (Leads and Review Team members) are volunteers committed to maintaining a strong and healthy community. Neighbors who are _not_ moderators will _not_ be able to see a list or any indication that someone is a moderator.

**Ways a neighbor may have become a moderator:**

1. The Founding Member of a neighborhood automatically gets Lead status once their neighborhood launches with 10 verified members.
2. In recently launched neighborhoods, the most active inviters may receive Lead status.
3. Nextdoor Support can appoint new moderators when there is a moderation need.

### Appealing a Moderation Decision

- **Authors:** Content authors can appeal a removal decision. Read more: [My content was removed on Nextdoor](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/my-content-was-reported?language=en_US).
- **Reporters:** If moderators choose not to remove content, the reporter can appeal the decision to a different set of moderators.

If you're concerned about a moderator in your area, please report their profile to Nextdoor Support using the _History of inconsistent moderation decisions_ reason. Read more: [How to report a neighbor](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/How-to-report-a-member?language=en_US).

## Community Programs Code of Conduct (Depth 2)

Nextdoor members can opt into roles that grant them access to additional tools and resources. Current community program roles include: Neighborhood Leads, Community Reviewers, and Welcome team members.

### Code of Conduct

1. **Be a good neighbor:** Treat Nextdoor members and fellow program participants with respect. Zero-tolerance policy towards harassment, profanity, discrimination, racism or any other abusive behavior.

2. **Model the community guidelines:** Be familiar with and act in accordance with each [guideline](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/community-guidelines?language=en_US). Those who deliberately act against the guidelines will be removed from the program.

3. **Be inclusive:** Our differences and diverse backgrounds allow for fresh perspectives. Anyone who does not follow the [do not discriminate policy](https://help.nextdoor.com/s/article/Do-not-discriminate?language=en_US) will be removed from the program.

4. **Maintain confidentiality:** Keep reported content and Nearby Reviewer conversations confidential, as well as the voting history of other program participants.

5. **Respect Neighbor Privacy:** Honor the privacy choices of members, including their decisions to use block or mute features. Refrain from maintaining any records of members' behaviors.

### Examples of Reasons for Program Removal

- Biased reporting of other members, including for reasons related to race or microaggressions
- Biased moderation by encouraging reporting of content from outside a moderator's local area
- Receiving multiple mutes or reports from neighbors
- Sharing confidential information (voting history, private conversations)
- A pattern of reporting neighbors or content that does not violate the guidelines
- Overtly racist commentary or behavior
- Aggressive or abusive behavior towards Nextdoor employees, vendors, or partners

## See Your Moderation History (Depth 2)

Nextdoor's moderation history tool helps Review Team members and Neighborhood Leads review reported content from the past, see what content still needs review, and understand what content has been removed and why.

### 1. Moderation History Dashboard

- **Reports:** Shows the number of reported messages within the date range selected.
- **Resolved:** Shows the number of reported messages that were resolved (either kept or removed).
- **Your votes:** How many messages you voted on.
- **Date picker:** Adjusting the date picker updates the dashboard with numbers from that date range.

### 2. Filter Tabs

- **All (default):** Shows all reported content you could have voted on.
- **Your votes:** Shows only the content you have voted on.
- **Kept:** Shows content that received enough "Keep votes" to close.
- **Remove:** Shows content that received enough "Remove votes" to be removed.
- **Pending:** Shows content reported less than 15 days ago still open to voting.

### 3. Voting History Detail

Clicking on the number of votes shows the voting history for that message, including:

- **Status:** Kept, Removed, Pending, or Active
- **Removal reasons:** The author removed, Reviewers voted to remove, A lead removed, Removed by a combination of votes and automated systems, Removed by Nextdoor, or Original post removed
- Vote totals
- How different moderators voted (and their comments)
- Who reported the content and why
